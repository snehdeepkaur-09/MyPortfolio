Robotic Nurse

At Innovation Fest 2025, our team Care Squad built a smart, AI-powered Robotic Nurse — a healthcare assistant robot designed to reduce NHS staff workload, promote hygiene, and automate medicine delivery in hospitals.


My Contributions:

  • Designed a 3D-printed frame with a sleek, modular structure and team branding.
  • Created impactful visual posters to showcase features and impact at the event.
  • Programmed Arduino for automated cleaning brushes and servo-controlled medicine box.

Core Features:

  • Dual rotating brushes + dust collector for hospital floor cleaning.
  • Servo-controlled smart medicine box triggered by pushbutton.
  • Autonomous navigation using ultrasonic sensors for safe movement.
  • Dual Arduino boards for multitasking & efficiency.
  • 6 DC motors, 2 motor drivers for smooth drive mechanism.

Technologies Used:

Arduino Uno (x2), Ultrasonic Sensors, DC Motors (x6), Motor Drivers, Servo Motors, Pushbutton, Breadboard, 3D Printed Frame.


Impact Goals:

  • Reduce manual workload for NHS staff.
  • Improve hygiene and patient safety.
  • Ensure timely medicine delivery during peak hours.
  • Contribute to UN SDGs for Health and Well-being.

This project proves how student-led innovation can solve real-world problems. With empathy and engineering, we created a functional prototype ready to make a difference in healthcare.

Space Based Solar Power

As one of the Top 8 in the Viasat: Space for Good UK University Challenge, we proposed a cutting-edge solution to global energy poverty: Space-Based Solar Power (SBSP) enhanced by AI. Our idea addressed the issue of 750+ million people lacking electricity and aimed to offer 24/7, clean, weather-proof energy from space using microwave beam transmission.


The system captures solar energy via satellites in Low Earth Orbit (LEO), converts it to microwaves, and beams it safely to Earth-based rectennas. Unlike traditional renewables, SBSP works continuously, unaffected by night or weather.


My Contributions:

  • Led research on LEO vs GEO satellite systems and proposed a low-cost, MVP-first approach.
  • Created risk mitigation plans (cybersecurity, cost, regulation, space debris).
  • Conducted data analysis using Google Colab, leveraging Pandas and Seaborn to explore and visualize key metrics.
  • Used Canva for 3D visual design, graphs, and functional diagrams.
  • Produced a compelling video pitch using visual storytelling and video editing tools.
  • Analyzed financial models (£1.5B+ full scale vs MVP cost of £170M–500M).

Innovation:

What set my idea apart was the integration of AI algorithms to optimize satellite beam targeting, position control, and energy demand forecasting in real time—improving reliability, efficiency, and scalability.


Impact:

This project aligns with UN Sustainable Development Goal 7 (Affordable and Clean Energy) and aims to improve access to energy for 100M+ people by 2035.


What I Gained:

I gained deep insights into the intersection of aerospace, AI, energy policy, and sustainability. It strengthened my skills in presenting high-stakes ideas and building tech solutions with real-world impact.
